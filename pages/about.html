Author.

Hey there! Thank you for dedicating your time to reading and skimming through this website. I am Jen and I am currently a sophomore student at Northbridge International School of Cambodia (NISC). I am so proud that this website turned out very well after all the effort and mental energy I've dedicated to creating this online cookbook. Throughout the production process, I'm very grateful for all the support and advice I received from those who've helped me during my hard times including my family, friends, and supervisor. This book consists of 10 authentic Khmer local recipes that I and my family used to enjoy back in the days when COVID wasn't a thing. I hope that this website will help you develop your cooking skills as well as learn more about the Khmer local cuisine and the beauty of our culture. Thank you!

FAQs.

  1. Why did you choose these dishes?
    2. My goal was to write recipes for ONLY authentic local dishes that were made and preserved for the younger generations of the Khmer people. According to my research regarding our local foods, there are a variety of dishes that weren't actually made by our ancestors. They are either from neighboring countries or influenced by our colonizers back in the old days. As I've talked to the elders, these dishes were actually made by our ancestors with some influences from other nations. Thatâ€™s why I chose these dishes for my website.
  2. Which dish did you like the best (or the least) why?
    3. I think that my favorite dish has to be Songkya or the Cambodian-styled pumpkin custard. Since pumpkin is one of my favorite fruits, I really like the sweet and creamy taste of this dish and I like how it's very easy and simple to recreate the dish as it requires little ingredients that could be found anywhere.
  3. How long did it take me to make this website?
    4. It took me like 6 to 7 months just to get everything done. However, it shouldn't take that long if you're highly motivated and committed to finishing everything in a short period.
  4. What are the challenges you faced while programming the website?
    5. Other than the lack of motivation and frequent procrastination, I find that figuring out and estimating the size of the font and the space or gap of each text, image, shape, etc. would be the most challenging part of the website construction process. It has brought me so many headaches and mental breakdowns. Another constraint I remember was having to fix errors and misplacements of the codes. I had to ask my brother for help.
  5. What did you use to take images of your dishes? What platform did you use to edit them?
    6. I personally used my iPhone 11 pro to capture all the images of the dishes I made. I also utilized the gallery edit function of the iPhone to adjust the lighting and brightness of the pictures to make them look more appealing and professional. As for editing/adding scribbles to those pictures, I did it on Canva.
  6. Did you make this entire website alone? Who taught you how to make these dishes?
    7. To be very honest, no, I didn't make this entire website alone. I had a lot of guidance and help from my brother as it was very hard for me to manage and work on everything on my own. As for cooking, I had my nanny and my grandma to guide me on making those recipes and I got a little help from youtube as well.
